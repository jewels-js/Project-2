In recent months -- at a scale I've hardly seen before in my short life, -- violent videos, sensational stories and pre-recorded rants are populating feeds all over the nation and, at its core, most of them tug at one root: Latinidad.


What is Latinidad?


It is the idea of community. It is the self-identifier created for, albeit contested by some, to unify Latine communities. It is the identifier by which people of Latin and Carribean descent can cling to for shared social, cultural, and even political commonalities.

Quite literally, Latinidad is the Spanish word for "Latino-ness." In a day, where some people are treating your Latino-ness as a target, it might be worthwhile to explore the creative ways others are using this identifier to express themselves and share their stories.

How are Latines using their voices? How are they sharing their culture?

Art. Film. Music. Stories.
And they are doing amazing things in those mediums. Explore some of these creations with us!

Timeline of Contributions:

  • 1939: Diosa Costello becomes first Latina on Broadway
  • 1940s: Margaret Villa becomes one of the first female professional baseball players
  • 1950s: Celia Cruz pioneers a new age of salsa music to American audiences nationwide
  • 1960s: Chicano Civil Rights Movement
  • 1981: Carolina Herrera launches iconic clothing brand named after herself
  • 1993: Ellen Ochoa becomes first Latina to travel space
  • 2009: Sonia Sotomayor becomes first Latina and third woman to serve on the US Supreme Court
